Solutions include governmental increase in anti-trafficking law enforcement (which is inadequate at this time), focus on rescuing children or younger generations, providing education as a means of prevention, economically defunding the billion dollar business that is sex trafficking, etc.
Other non-profits working in this field include Apne Aap, Angels in the Field, Rescue Foundation, and Prerana.

None of these solutions feel similar to what I might design. Rahab’s Rope is the only non-profit organization I have found that directly sells (in America) the work that rescued women and children make in India. All of these organizations focus on educating women and children, providing healthcare, training in jobs, and much more, which is excellent and necessary work. Rahab’s Rope stands unique in that it provides this support hand-in-hand with awareness to other countries of this problem India is facing, and it does so with the direct handprint of products made by the people facing these deeply ingrained problems.
